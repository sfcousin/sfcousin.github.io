<p>
Crystallization processes are ubiquitous in living organisms. NMR spectroscopy appears as a great tool to study solid transformations, as it can deal with amorphous phases and can reach atomic-scale resolution. While NMR spectroscopy is limited by the signal intensity, MAS-DNP can help recovery sensitivity but is limited by the required specific sample preparation methods. This project aims at developing an automatic stop-flow device compatible with MAS-DNP for the observation of the crystallization processes ex situ. This technique will be tested on a process of high interest for biomineralization: CaCO3 crystallization. Once developed, this methodology will help transform MAS-DNP into a broad, versatile method for atomic-level observation of transient stages in systems evolving with time – with a focus on determining the role of additives in polymorph selection, and on the structural organization from nano- to micro-scales.

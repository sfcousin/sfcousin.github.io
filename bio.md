Bio
===


I was trained as a chemist at the *École Normale Supérieure* in Paris, studying mainly organic chemistry and a bit of physics.
As parts of the curriculum, I did three experimental internships under the mentoring of:

* **Anna Proust** in the *Laboratoire de Chimie des Métaux de Transition* (Université Pierre et Marie Curie, Paris), where I worked on organometallic and polyoxometalates.
* **Luminita Duma** and **Geoffrey Bodenhausen** in the lab *Laboratoire des biomolécules* (École Normale Supérieure, Paris), studying solid-state NMR of proteins.  
* **Antony Gee**  in the division of Imaging Sciences (Kings College London, London), studying solid-state Ugi reaction to the incorporation of PET tracer (nitrogen-13)  
* **Dimitrios Sakellariou**  in the lab *IRAMIS* (CEA, PARIS), studying MRI in rotating field. 

I completed my PhD under the supervision of **Fabien Ferrage** at the *Laboratoire des biomolécules* (ENS, Paris).
There, I worked on measurement, theory and analysis of various spin relaxation rate. First I help to develop a new hardware (2F-NMR) for the accurate measurement of low field relaxation rate, with spectral resolution. Then, I work on developing software for easy determination of spin-relaxation rate in various spin systems (RedKite). This analytical program has been used in the context of CDDH methyl groups of protein, to determine protein sidechain motions. 

My training does not end here, since as a Postdoctoral Research Associate in [**Lucio Frydman**'s group](https://www.weizmann.ac.il/chembiophys/Frydman_group/home) at the Weizmann Institute of Science (Rehovot, Israel), I extended my field of expertise to MRI spectroscopy, with the goal of implementing time frequency encoding into clinical MRI.
I then started a second postdoctoral research associate position in the laboratory in [**Sami Jannin**'s group](https://www.hmrlab.eu/) at the CRMN (Lyon, France), where I developed dissolution Dynamic Nuclear Polarization (d-DNP), in particular with the goal of using spin diffusion to slow down relaxation process and use hyperpolarized solution remotely. 
Finally, my last postdocal position was in [**Giulia Mollica**'s Laboratory](http://mollicalab.fr/) where I used MAS-DNP to study crystal formation. 

I have been received at the CNRS (section 16: Chimie et vivant) to explore how NMR can be used to extract sub-microstructure of various materials.
